Recognizing Warning Signs

Problem gambling often develops gradually. It can start with spending more than planned, chasing losses, hiding activity from family or feeling stressed when not playing. Noticing these signs early is important because limits and breaks work best before behaviour becomes difficult to control.

  • Trying to win back losses with larger bets.
  • Using money meant for bills or essentials.
  • Hiding gambling activity from people close to you.
  • Feeling anxious, angry or restless because of gambling.
  • Borrowing money to continue playing.
  • Ignoring time limits or budget limits repeatedly.

If You Need More Support

If gambling is affecting your finances, relationships, work or mental health, consider speaking with a professional support service. Platform tools can help with account control, but serious harm needs outside help from trained organizations or counselors.

  1. Stop depositing and take a break from gambling activity.
  2. Use account limits or self-exclusion if available.
  3. Talk to someone you trust about what is happening.
  4. Contact a professional gambling-support organization.
  5. Ask support to help lock or restrict the account if needed.
